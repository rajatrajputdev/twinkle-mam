Empanneled Jury for the 16th Tribal Youth Exchange Program by Home Ministry of India

I am deeply honored to have been a part of the Esteemed panel of jury members for the 16th Tribal Youth Exchange Program (TYEP) conducted by the Ministry of Culture & Sports, Government of India. The Event took place at Gandhi Smriti and Darshan Samiti, Rajghat, on February 24, 2025, and brought together 200 youth from various tribal regions across India, accompanied by officers from the CRPF. The program, spanning from February 19 to 25, 2025, was designed to foster cultural exchange, awareness, and exposure to educational and industrial advancements. One of the highlights was the Cultural Competition, where talented young participants showcased their rich traditions, artistic expressions, and vibrant heritage. Serving as a jury member for this prestigious event was a privilege, allowing me to witness the passion, creativity, and resilience of India's tribal youth. Their performances reflected the depth of their cultural roots while embracing new aspirations. I extend my gratitude to the Government of India and the Organizing commitee NYKS for this remarkable opportunity and commend their efforts in empowering tribal communities. Such initiatives pave the way for inclusion, growth, and national unity, reinforcing the beauty of India's diverse cultural fabric.

Participation in International Asian Dance Summit 2024

I am honored to have been a part of the International Asian Dance Summit 2024, held from December 28th to 31st in the vibrant city of Kathmandu, Nepal. This prestigious event brought together extraordinary dancers, scholars, and enthusiasts from across Asia, celebrating the rich and diverse traditions of classical and contemporary dance. As a judge for Bharatanatyam, Kuchipudi, Odissi, and Bollywood dance, I had the privilege of evaluating some of the finest talents in the field. It was an enriching experience to witness and assess performances that showcased the depth, precision, and grace of these celebrated dance forms. I was also fortunate to work alongside esteemed Indian classical dance personalities, including Rajendra Gangani Ji and Kathak Guru Sri Subhash Chandra Ji from Kathak Kendra, Delhi. Adding to this momentous occasion, I am deeply humbled to have been honored with the Nrutya Gurumani Award by ANASS India. This prestigious accolade recognizes my contributions and dedication to the art of dance, further inspiring me to continue my journey in preserving and promoting classical traditions. The International Asian Dance Summit 2024 served as a remarkable platform for cultural exchange, fostering deeper appreciation and dialogue among artists from diverse backgrounds. My experience at this event has reinforced my commitment to nurturing and advancing the legacy of classical and contemporary dance forms on a global stage. I am grateful for this opportunity to contribute to an international celebration of artistic excellence and look forward to many more such inspiring engagements in the world of dance.

Celebrating Culture and Heritage: My Experience at Sanskriti Sangam 2022

In 2022, I had the incredible honor of being a Guest of Honor at the grand cultural festival, Sanskriti Sangam 2022, hosted by NEV Events and Entertainment Pvt. Ltd. This prestigious event was the 6th International and 10th National Art, Instrumental, Yoga, and Music Dance Competition Festival, held from July 16th to July 31st at the iconic ShilpaGram Auditorium in Guwahati, Assam. The event was a spectacular celebration of India’s rich cultural diversity, bringing together talented artists, performers, and cultural enthusiasts from across the nation and around the globe. Sanskriti Sangam 2022 was not just a competition but a magnificent confluence of creativity, artistry, and heritage that showcased the vibrant traditions of India. One of the most memorable aspects of this event was the privilege of working alongside the legendary Assamese superstar Sankar Kalita, a name synonymous with excellence in the Assamese entertainment industry. Renowned for his contributions to Assamese cinema and his deep passion for preserving Assamese culture, Sankar Kalita’s presence elevated the festival to new heights. It was inspiring to witness his dedication to promoting the arts and engaging with talented individuals from various disciplines. Additionally, the event brought together several distinguished personalities from the fields of Indian art, music, and dance, making it a melting pot of cultural brilliance. Being part of this festival allowed me to witness breathtaking performances across multiple art forms, from soulful instrumental music and dynamic dance performances to awe-inspiring yoga showcases. Beyond the performances, my participation in Sanskriti Sangam 2022 was a profound opportunity to connect with India’s artistic legacy. It underscored the importance of cultural unity and the role of the arts in bringing people together. Engaging with artists from diverse backgrounds was not only humbling but also a powerful reminder of the transformative power of culture. Looking back, Sanskriti Sangam 2022 remains a defining highlight of my journey as a cultural advocate. It reaffirmed my commitment to celebrating and supporting the arts while embracing India’s timeless traditions. This extraordinary event stands as a testament to the enduring beauty of Indian culture and its ability to inspire and unite us all.

Performing for a World Leader: A Defining Moment in My Journey

One of the most unforgettable milestones of my life took place in the year 1989, when I had the extraordinary opportunity to perform an Indian cultural dance in front of none other than Mikhail Gorbachev, the then President of the USSR, at the Indo-Russian Embassy. What made this moment even more special was that I was just 14 years old, a young girl from the small city of Saharanpur, Uttar Pradesh, stepping onto an international stage. Coming from a humble background, this achievement was a testament to the power of dreams, hard work, and the unyielding support of my mentors and family. Performing at such a prestigious event and being acknowledged for showcasing the essence of Indian culture to a global leader was a profoundly humbling experience. It wasn't just a dance performance—it was a celebration of India's rich heritage, bridging cultures, and creating a moment of harmony between two great nations. This experience instilled in me a deep sense of pride in my cultural roots and a commitment to using art as a means of connection and unity. Even decades later, I cherish this achievement as a defining moment that shaped my journey as an artist and as an individual. Looking back, it feels surreal to have had the honor of representing my country at such a young age and contributing to the cultural dialogue between India and Russia. This moment remains etched in my heart as a beacon of inspiration and proof that no dream is too big, no matter where you come from.
